Frequently Asked Questions (Retinal Detachment)

What causes retinal detachment?

Retinal detachment is caused by a combination of factors including retinal holes, breaks, or tears; liquefaction of the vitreous humor; and mechanical forces (traction) on the retina.

What is the relationship between near-sightedness and retinal detachment?

While being near-sighted doesn't directly cause detachment, certain peripheral retinal degenerations (like lattice degeneration) are more common in near-sighted individuals and can lead to retinal holes or tears, increasing the risk.

Can retinal detachment cause total blindness?

Yes, if left untreated, a retinal detachment can progress and lead to permanent and total vision loss in the affected eye.

What treatments are used for retinal detachment repair?

The primary treatments are surgical and include Vitrectomy, Scleral Buckle, and Pneumatic Retinopexy (gas bubble injection). Laser or cryotherapy is also used to seal the retinal breaks.

Is surgery the only option?

For a retinal detachment, surgery is almost always necessary. Medications or eye drops cannot reattach the retina. In some cases of small holes or tears without detachment, laser treatment may be sufficient to prevent a detachment from occurring.

Post-Operative Instructions

  • There are generally no diet restrictions, except for diabetic patients who should follow their prescribed diet.
  • Avoid all possible injuries to the operated eye for at least two months. This includes not playing with children.
  • Attend all post-operative check-ups as instructed by the doctor.
  • Head baths or showers are typically permitted after two days, but please confirm with your doctor.
  • Gently wipe any discharge around the eye with a clean, cotton swab soaked in warm water.
  • Some minimal watering and redness is normal for up to 8 weeks.
  • Normal household activities, including cooking, can be resumed.
  • If you experience decreasing vision or significant pain, report to the doctor immediately.
  • Use dark glasses as instructed to protect your eye from bright light.
  • Take all prescribed medicines exactly as instructed.
