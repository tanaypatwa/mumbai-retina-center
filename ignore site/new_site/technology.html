State-of-the-Art Technology

Investing in world-class equipment for the most precise diagnostics and treatments.

Carl Zeiss HD-OCT & Operating Microscope (Germany)

The HD-OCT provides high-resolution, cross-sectional images of the retina, allowing for early detection and monitoring of diseases like macular degeneration and diabetic retinopathy. The Zeiss operating microscope offers unparalleled magnification and clarity during delicate vitreoretinal surgeries, enhancing safety and precision.

Heidelberg Confocal Imaging System (Germany)

This advanced system provides detailed, multi-modal imaging of the retina and optic nerve. It is crucial for diagnosing and managing a wide range of conditions, including glaucoma and retinal vascular diseases, by offering different imaging modes like fluorescein angiography and autofluorescence.

Storz Vitrectomy & Phaco Systems (USA)

The Storz system is a cornerstone of our surgical suite. It allows for minimally invasive vitrectomy surgery to treat conditions like retinal detachment and diabetic retinopathy. Its integrated phacoemulsification capability also enables safe and efficient cataract removal during complex combination procedures.

Quantel Iridis Laser (France)

This versatile laser platform is used for a variety of therapeutic procedures, including retinal photocoagulation for diabetic retinopathy and sealing retinal tears. Its precision and safety features ensure that treatment is targeted effectively, preserving surrounding healthy tissue.

Sonomed Ultrasound Systems (USA)

When direct visualization of the back of the eye is obscured by blood or a dense cataract, high-frequency ultrasound provides a clear picture of the retinal structure. It is an essential diagnostic tool for assessing retinal detachments and other posterior segment abnormalities.
